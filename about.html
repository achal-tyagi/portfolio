Achal Tyagi

I am currently an MSc Management Consultancy student at UCD Michael Smurfit Graduate Business School. My professional focus sits at the intersection of rigorous analytical thought and real-world implementation, ranging from defining global market entry strategies for start-ups to optimizing operational efficiency and client engagement for established firms.

I earned my Bachelor of Commerce (Hons.) at Vivekananda Institute of Professional Studies, India with a CGPA of 8.42 (First Division). During my time there and my subsequent role as a Financial Analyst, I discovered a passion for identifying performance gaps and translating complex data into actionable business objectives. Beyond academics, organizing a 150-person collegiate football tournament and successfully pitching a full-funding sponsorship proposal to Red Bull taught me the value of collaborative communication and commercial awareness. These formative experiences pulled me firmly towards a career in consulting.

Consulting chose me as much as I chose it. The pivot point was when i applied Lean Six Sigma principles to untangle complex operational bottlenecks, it showed me that data is only as good as the strategy it informs. Seeing a 15% improvement in efficiency from a single workflow redesign proved that structured, dedicated support is the most direct way to amplify a company’s impact. This insight, that good strategy builds lasting value has stayed with me through every project since, from conducting deep-dive competitor benchmarking to architecting market entry roadmaps. For me, consulting is about that specific moment of clarity: when a messy operational challenge is translated into a streamlined, high-growth objective.
